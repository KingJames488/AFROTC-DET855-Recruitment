About AFROTC

The Air Force ROTC (Reserve Officer Training Corps) is a program that trains college/university students to be officers in the Air Force while they still are in school. At BYU this program automatically gives every Cadet a minor in Aerospace Studies and fully prepares them for military life. Besides the few required Air Force classes, you can have a regular major, go to all the regular classes, and have a regular college experience, all while you prepare for the adventure of a lifetime after graduation.

To participate in the program, you must register for "AEROS" classes including PT (Physical Training), LLAB (Leadership Lab), and a one-credit hour class that teaches Air Force principles, military history/strategy, and leadership techniques.

There is no obligation to stay for the first two years (Freshman and Sophomores), so you can get a feel for military life without the full commitment. If you decide you want to join, you will commission after graduation and be a full-time officer of the greatest Air Force in the world.
